Unpaired Modality Translation for Pseudo Labeling of Histology Images

MICCAI 2024
Arthur Boschet, Armand Collin, Nishka Katoch, Julien Cohen-Adad
PyTorch GANs Domain Adaptation Computer Vision Medical Imaging Python
Paper

Addressing the critical challenge of limited annotated data in biomedical imaging, I developed a microscopy pseudo-labeling pipeline using unsupervised image translation techniques. The system generated high-quality pseudo labels by translating between labeled and unlabeled domains without requiring prior annotation in the target domain. By implementing a SyncDiff-based domain adaptation pipeline with VQ-VAE compression, I improved segmentation accuracy on out-of-distribution microscopy data by 12% while accelerating training speeds and maintaining 95% image fidelity.

The method was validated across three increasingly dissimilar image domains, achieving a mean Dice score of 0.736 ± 0.005 on Scanning Electron Microscopy (SEM) datasets using synthetic data translated from Transmission Electron Microscopy (TEM). This approach significantly accelerated the annotation process by providing researchers with refined starting points for manual labeling, bridging the gap between different microscopy modalities in biomedical research.

Multi-contrast Image-to-Image Translation for Axon and Myelin Segmentation

Master's Thesis
Nishka Katoch, Julien Cohen-Adad, Guy Wolf
Diffusion Models PyTorch Image Translation Privacy-Preserving AI Medical Imaging Segmentation Python
Thesis

To address data scarcity in neurodegenerative disease research, I developed a privacy-preserving image translation framework using latent diffusion models for histological image analysis. The system converted source images into latent encodings and reconstructed them across different modalities using guided diffusion models, with segmentation masks integrated directly into the latent space. This architecture enabled domain adaptation without requiring access to real patient data, ensuring compliance with medical privacy regulations.

The framework achieved an 80% Dice score using synthetic contrast data, demonstrating effective segmentation performance on neurological tissue across diverse electron microscopy modalities. By minimizing the number of translation models required and operating entirely on latent encodings, this approach offered a scalable solution for studying Alzheimer's disease and multiple sclerosis while maintaining strict data privacy standards.

SpeechBrain Scaling Study

Research Project
Nishka Katoch*, Artem Ploujnikov* (* = equal contribution)
Speech Recognition Transformers Scaling Laws PyTorch NLU Self-Supervised Learning Python
Report

I conducted a comprehensive empirical study on the scaling properties of speech recognition and natural language understanding models in multispeaker settings. The research examined CTC-based speech recognition with pretrained wav2vec2 encoders and a bimodal NLU intent classification model, comparing different audio preprocessing approaches including raw features, RAVE variational autoencoder latents, and wav2vec2 acoustic representations. Through systematic benchmarking, I derived predictive scaling laws: power laws for ASR and audio accuracy, and logarithmic laws for text-to-audio embedding similarity.

The findings revealed that wav2vec2-based models followed predictable scaling patterns with optimal performance at specific model widths and layer counts. Pretrained acoustic models demonstrated superior scaling compared to training from scratch, with wav2vec2 offering the greatest advantage. These insights directly informed efficient deployment strategies for multilingual ASR systems in low-resource environments, helping organizations optimize model selection based on available data and computational constraints.

Single-Cell Transcriptomic Visualization and Analysis of Alzheimer's Disease

Research Project
Shuang Ni*, Nishka Katoch*, Timur Zhanabaev* (* = equal contribution)
scRNA-seq Analysis Bioinformatics Python Data Visualization UMAP Scanpy Clustering
Report

I led a comprehensive analysis of single-nucleus transcriptomes from the prefrontal cortex of 48 individuals to understand the cellular context of Alzheimer's disease pathology. Using six different dimensionality reduction and visualization methods—including Truncated TSVD, kernel PCA, Isomaps, t-SNE, UMAP, and PHATE—I revealed distinct geometrical structures in cell-type distributions and identified fine-grain patterns correlating with AD progression markers (CERAD scores and Braak stages).

The analysis uncovered cell-type-specific pathological associations, identifying Mic1 as an AD-associated subpopulation and Oli1 as a no-pathology subpopulation. Visualizations revealed a gender bias in AD diagnosis prevalence among females, aligning with findings in existing literature. This multi-scale approach to genetic and clinical feature exploration provides a foundation for identifying AD-pathology-associated cell populations and potential biomarkers for early disease detection.

Hybrid Quantum-Classical Models for Deepfake Image Detection

Quantum ML Research Intern
Tech Mahindra, Maker's Lab
Quantum Machine Learning Quantum Computing CaiT (Vision Transformers) Parameterized Quantum Circuits Binary Cross-Entropy Optimization Deepfake Detection PyTorch

Developed a novel hybrid classical-quantum architecture for detecting AI-generated synthetic images by leveraging Parameterized Quantum Circuits (PQC) for binary classification. The system combines traditional deep learning feature extraction with quantum neural networks to identify manipulated content from contemporary generative models including Flux, DALL-E, and Gemini.

Implemented a multi-stage pipeline featuring Class Attention Vision Transformer (CaiT) for feature extraction, reducing high-dimensional image data to 64-dimensional feature vectors suitable for quantum processing. Designed and evaluated quantum circuits with 4 and 8 qubits using Hadamard gates for superposition, Rotation-Y gates for parameterization, and CNOT gates for entanglement. Explored two architectural approaches: a linear 8-qubit circuit duplicating the 4-qubit structure, and a hierarchical circuit with cross-entanglement distinguishing between coarse and fine feature groups for multi-scale learning.

The research addressed critical challenges at the classical-quantum interface, including dimensionality reduction, quantum encoding strategies, and circuit depth optimization. This work demonstrates the potential of quantum computing to enhance pattern recognition in deepfake detection while identifying practical deployment considerations for hybrid quantum-classical systems in computer vision applications across financial services, identity verification, e-commerce, and legal compliance.
